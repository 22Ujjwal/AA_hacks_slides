Good morning. We're Ujjwal, Itay, and Natalia — and over the next seven minutes we'll walk you through a risk-prediction system for American's crew sequences at DFW.
The Problem
Crew Sequence
One tail. Two flights. One failure point.
Inbound A→DFW then DFW→B on the same aircraft, 30–240 min apart. One weather event disrupts the entire rotation.
Disruption Cost
Weather + NAS: DFW's top cancel drivers.
Every cancelled rotation cascades — hotels, rebookings, misconnects, NPS hits.
11.4% DFW disruption rate
The Gap
Schedulers fly blind.
Zero per-sequence risk signal. High-risk and low-risk rotations get identical treatment.
Disruption Cascade
A crew sequence is one aircraft tail flying A→DFW then DFW→B. When weather hits the whole rotation cascades. We built a calibrated risk signal.

Data Sources & Sequence Construction
Source 1 — BTS On-Time Performance
Years2015–2024 (COVID 2020–21 excluded)
CarriersAll carriers, DFW-filtered
Records~18M flight-level rows
FieldsWeatherDelay, ArrDelay, Tail_Number, CancellationCode, NASDelay
Ground truth for delays, cancellations, reason codes.
Source 2 — NOAA Global Summary of Month
CoverageAll airports with CDO stations
Gap55% of airports have no station → median imputation
Featuresavg_wind_speed, precip_days, extreme_precip, max_wind_gust
Cached — no API call at runtime.
Source 3 — Open-Meteo ERA5 Reanalysis
Coverage100% of airports — global ERA5 reanalysis, no station gaps
Period2015–2024 daily → aggregated to monthly
Featuresavg_wind_mph, max_gust_mph, total_precip_in, precip_days, severe_wx_days, thunder_days, snow_days
APIarchive-api.open-meteo.com · free · no key required
Replaces GSOM median imputation — full airport coverage at inference time.
